# AI Summary — EASER Research Proposal

## Objective
The project's response to this multifactorial challenge involves fostering interdisciplinary collaboration among Chilean universities,
private institutions, government agencies, and international partners. EASER aims to develop a methodology for assessing the time
evolution of seismic risk, focusing on residential buildings within the Andean seismotectonic environment. The resulting
computational tools will empower stakeholders to make rational, time-dependent risk-management decisions, bridging the gap left
by conventional stationary assessments and ensuring the resilience of assets in the face of evolving seismic hazards.

## Methodology
Our goal is to assemble a cohesive, multidisciplinary group of researchers to advance the knowledge on the time dependency of
seismic risk and to promote an environment that fosters collaboration and innovation, providing an international academic
environment to train young earthquake scientists at the highest level. We will integrate seismology, geotechnical, and structural
engineering to quantify the interrelation between seismic hazard time-dependency, seismic vulnerability time-dependency, and the
exposure evolution over time, with epistemic and aleatory uncertainty considered in every step. Our results will ultimately allow the
development of advanced computational tools to aid both public and private stakeholders in assessing more realistically the seismic
risk in residential structures and communities within the Andean seismotectonic environment and making informed decisions to
mitigate these risks.
